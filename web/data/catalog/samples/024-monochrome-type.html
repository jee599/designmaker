About

Less, then less again.

Studio Null is one person working at the intersection of typography and interaction. Based in Seoul, working globally. Every project starts by removing what doesn't earn its place on the page.

Former design lead at a company you haven't heard of. Left to do work that's more honest. Clients come through referrals only — no portfolio deck, no pitch process. The work speaks or it doesn't.

Tools: Figma for wireframes, code for everything else. No design system — every project gets its own rules, then I follow them obsessively.

Process

01

Listen

A one-hour call. No deck. I ask what you're building, who it's for, and what keeps you up at night about the current version. Usually the real brief emerges 40 minutes in.

02

Reduce

I take the brief and cut it in half. Then cut it again. What remains goes into a one-page design direction — typography scale, color (or lack of it), layout rhythm. We align before a single pixel is placed.

03

Build

Design and code happen simultaneously. I don't hand off Figma files — I build the real thing. Two review rounds. Ship date is set on day one and doesn't move.

04

Refine

After launch, one week of tuning. Kerning adjustments, animation timing, responsive edge cases. The 2% that separates good from precise.
